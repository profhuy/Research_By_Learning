\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

This section reports the quantitative outcomes of the pilot experiment.
All values are computed from \texttt{src/compute\_metrics.py} using the
data in \texttt{data/pilot\_ground\_truth.csv} and
\texttt{results/pilot\_llm\_output.csv} ($N = 30$ issues).

% ----------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{RQ1: Human–Human Inter-Annotator Agreement}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:hh_kappa} shows Cohen's Kappa between the two human
annotators (Annotator~1 and Annotator~2) across the three components.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Human–Human Cohen's Kappa (pilot, $N=30$)}
\label{tab:hh_kappa}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\hline
\textbf{Component} & \textbf{Kappa} & \textbf{Interpretation} & \textbf{Decision} \\
\hline
OB  & 1.000 & Almost perfect & PASS ($\geq 0.6$) \\
EB  & 1.000 & Almost perfect & PASS ($\geq 0.6$) \\
S2R & 1.000 & Almost perfect & PASS ($\geq 0.6$) \\
\hline
\textbf{Overall} & \textbf{1.000} & Almost perfect & PASS ($\geq 0.6$) \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Raw agreement between the two annotators was 100\% across all components.
The overall Human–Human Kappa of 1.000 exceeds the accepted range of
0.6–0.8; this anomaly is noted in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}.

% ----------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{RQ2: LLM-vs-Developer Agreement}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:llm_kappa} shows Cohen's Kappa between the LLM output
(\texttt{GPT-4o mini}, temperature = 0.0, prompt v2) and the developer
consensus labels.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{LLM-vs-Developer Cohen's Kappa (pilot, $N=30$, 0 invalid)}
\label{tab:llm_kappa}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\hline
\textbf{Component} & \textbf{Kappa} & \textbf{Interpretation} & \textbf{Decision} \\
\hline
OB  & 1.000 & Almost perfect & PASS ($\geq 0.6$) \\
EB  & 0.695 & Substantial    & PASS ($\geq 0.6$) \\
S2R & 0.417 & Moderate       & FAIL ($< 0.6$)    \\
\hline
\textbf{Overall} & \textbf{0.618} & Substantial & PASS ($\geq 0.6$) \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Raw agreement between LLM output and developer consensus was 84.4\%.
The LLM achieved strong agreement on OB ($\kappa = 1.000$) and
acceptable agreement on EB ($\kappa = 0.695$), but fell below the
acceptance threshold on S2R ($\kappa = 0.417$).

% ----------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Label Distribution}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:label_dist} compares the distribution of labels assigned
by developer consensus versus the LLM across all three components
combined ($3 \times 30 = 90$ label slots).

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Label distribution: developer consensus vs.\ LLM (pilot)}
\label{tab:label_dist}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\hline
\textbf{Label}    & \textbf{Consensus} & \textbf{LLM} \\
\hline
Sufficient  & 64 & 69 \\
Ambiguous   &  3 &  5 \\
Missing     & 23 & 16 \\
Incorrect   &  0 &  0 \\
\hline
\textbf{Total} & \textbf{90} & \textbf{90} \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The LLM tends to assign \textit{Sufficient} more frequently than human
annotators (69 vs.\ 64), and assigns \textit{Missing} less frequently
(16 vs.\ 23). Neither annotators nor the LLM assigned the
\textit{Incorrect} label to any component in the pilot sample.

% ----------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Data Quality}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

All 30 pilot issues returned valid JSON responses from the LLM
($N_{\text{valid}} = 30$, $N_{\text{invalid}} = 0$).
No issues were excluded from the LLM-vs-Developer Kappa calculation.
